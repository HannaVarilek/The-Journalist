Clinic will soon offer vasectomy


By Nancy Stohs

Vasectomies soon will be available to low-income persons free of charge or for a small fee at the Planned Parenthood Center, 3830 Adams St.

The cutting of the sperm duct, which takes about 20 minutes, will be available by appointment one day a week, Steve White center director said.

Performed by a local urologist or other physician, the operation normally would cost from $100 to $150, he said.

Planned Parenthood closed its vasectomy clinic about six months ago because it ran out of funds, White said. It opened in March 1970 on a federal grant for a vasectomy counselor.

White said it is being reopened in about a month because he believes there’s a need for the operation among low-income persons.

The physician is volunteering his services, so the only expense to the center will be the cost of supplies, he said.

White came to Lincoln two months ago from a Planned Parenthood Center in Kansas City, Mo.

There, as well as in Nebraska, spouse consent isn’t legally required for either a vasectomy or tubal ligation, he said. Tubal ligation is the sterilization operation for women.

Most doctors, however, request it as general practice, to prevent marital problems afterward, he said.

Both operations are relatively simple, he said.

Only in a small percentage of cases is either operation reversible, he added.
